# California CTE Pathway Structures

## Overview

California's Career Technical Education (CTE) system is organized around **15 Industry Sectors**, each containing multiple **pathways** that sequence courses from introductory to advanced levels. CTE is a significant component of many district LCAPs, particularly under college and career readiness goals.

## Sectors Most Relevant to ModelIt

### Information and Communication Technologies (ICT)
**Pathways**:
- Information Support and Services
- Networking
- Software and Systems Development
- Games and Simulation (direct ModelIt alignment)

**ModelIt connection**: Computational modeling and simulation directly supports the Games and Simulation pathway and Software and Systems Development. Systems thinking and computational modeling are foundational ICT skills.

### Engineering and Architecture
**Pathways**:
- Architectural Design
- Engineering Design
- Engineering Technology
- Environmental Engineering

**ModelIt connection**: Engineering design processes rely on modeling and simulation. Environmental engineering connects to ecosystem and pollution modeling. The Engineering Design pathway emphasizes iterative design using models.

### Health Science and Medical Technology
**Pathways**:
- Biotechnology
- Health Care Administrative Services
- Health Care Operational Support
- Mental and Behavioral Health
- Patient Care
- Public and Community Health

**ModelIt connection**: Biotechnology and public health pathways benefit from biological systems modeling (disease spread, population dynamics, cellular processes). Biotech pathway students use computational tools for data analysis and simulation.

### Energy, Environment, and Utilities
**Pathways**:
- Energy and Power Technology
- Energy, Environment, and Utilities Maintenance
- Environmental Resources
- Telecommunications

**ModelIt connection**: Environmental Resources pathway directly aligns with ecosystem modeling, climate modeling, pollution/remediation modeling, and resource management simulations.

## How CTE Pathways Work

### Course Sequence Structure

CTE pathways follow a three-course sequence:

```
Introductory Course (9th-10th grade)
    |
    v
Concentrator Course (10th-11th grade)
    |
    v
Capstone Course (11th-12th grade)
```

**Definitions**:
- **Introductory**: Broad exposure to the sector; develops foundational knowledge
- **Concentrator**: Deeper skill development within a specific pathway; a student who completes this course is a "CTE Concentrator" (reported on the California Dashboard)
- **Capstone**: Advanced, often project-based; may include industry certification, work-based learning, or capstone project

### Student Progression Metrics
| Metric | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| **CTE Participant** | Enrolled in at least one CTE course |
| **CTE Concentrator** | Completed 2+ courses in a single pathway |
| **CTE Completer** | Completed the full pathway sequence (intro through capstone) |
| **CTE Pathway Completion Rate** | Percentage of concentrators who become completers |

### Work-Based Learning Continuum
CTE pathways integrate work-based learning (WBL) across a continuum:

1. **Career Awareness** (all students): Guest speakers, career fairs, field trips
2. **Career Exploration** (participants): Job shadows, informational interviews, career mentoring
3. **Career Preparation** (concentrators/completers): Internships, pre-apprenticeships, school-based enterprises
4. **Career Training** (advanced): Registered apprenticeships, clinical rotations

## CTE Funding in California

### Perkins V (Federal)
- **Carl D. Perkins Career and Technical Education Act**, reauthorized as "Strengthening Career and Technical Education for the 21st Century Act" (Perkins V, 2018)
- Federal formula grant distributed to states, then to local agencies
- Funds must support CTE programs that are "of sufficient size, scope, and quality"
- Requires a **Comprehensive Local Needs Assessment (CLNA)** every 2 years
- Key accountability indicators: concentrator/completer rates, placement, nontraditional participation
- Perkins funds often appear in LCAP Action Tables as a funding source for CTE actions

### CTEIG (State)
- **California Career Technical Education Incentive Grant**
- Competitive state grant program established by AB 2217 (2017), extended multiple times
- Awards up to $600 per student in CTE pathways
- Requires local match (1:1 for most districts, reduced for small/rural)
- Funds pathway development, equipment, WBL coordination, industry partnerships
- Application requires alignment to CTE standards and labor market data
- Very commonly referenced in LCAPs as a CTE funding source

### Strong Workforce Program (State)
- Primarily a community college program, but K-12 benefits through:
  - K-12 Strong Workforce partnership grants
  - Dual enrollment pathway articulation
  - Regional coordination through Strong Workforce Regional Consortia
- Funds career pathway alignment between K-12, community colleges, and industry
- Regional labor market data from Strong Workforce informs K-12 CTE pathway decisions

### K-12 Strong Workforce
- Specific K-12 allocation within Strong Workforce
- Supports CTE pathway development at the high school level
- Often administered through county offices of education
- Funds teacher externships, pathway launches, industry partnerships

### Other CTE Funding Sources
| Source | Type | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| LCFF Base | State | Districts can fund CTE from general LCFF |
| LCFF Supplemental/Concentration | State | If CTE targets unduplicated pupils |
| Title I | Federal | If CTE supports academic achievement for disadvantaged students |
| Title IV, Part A | Federal | Well-rounded education includes CTE |
| EEBG | State | Professional development for CTE teachers |
| Local bonds / Prop 51 | Local/State | Facilities and equipment |

## How CTE Connects to LCAP Goals

CTE typically appears in LCAPs under several goal areas:

### College and Career Readiness Goals
- **CTE pathway development**: Launching new or expanding existing pathways
- **A-G alignment**: Ensuring CTE courses meet UC/CSU A-G requirements
- **College/Career Indicator (CCI)**: CTE pathway completion is one way students can score "Prepared" on the CCI

### Academic Achievement Goals
- **CTE integration with core academics**: Using CTE context to teach math, science, ELA
- **Applied STEM learning**: CTE as a vehicle for hands-on STEM instruction
- **Cross-curricular projects**: CTE capstone projects integrating multiple subjects

### Equity and Access Goals
- **CTE for unduplicated pupils**: Supplemental/Concentration funds used to provide CTE access for underserved students
- **Nontraditional participation**: Increasing representation in pathways (e.g., women in engineering, men in health science)
- **CTE and EL students**: Bilingual CTE programs, sheltered CTE instruction

### Budget Signals in LCAPs
When analyzing LCAP Action Tables for CTE spending, look for:
- **CTEIG** or **Perkins** in the funding source column
- **CTE Coordinator** or **CTE Director** positions in personnel expenditures
- **Industry certification fees** in services/supplies line items
- **Equipment purchases** (4000s/6000s object codes) tagged to CTE
- **WBL Coordinator** positions
- **Advisory committee** references (CTE requires industry advisory committees)

## ModelIt Positioning Within CTE

ModelIt can enhance CTE pathways by providing:

1. **Computational modeling skills** that are transferable across ICT, engineering, biotech, and environmental pathways
2. **Systems thinking** as a foundational CTE competency (understanding how complex systems behave)
3. **NGSS Science and Engineering Practices** alignment, particularly "Developing and Using Models"
4. **Data literacy** through model-based data analysis and interpretation
5. **Cross-pathway integration** -- a single modeling tool that serves multiple CTE sectors
6. **Bilingual support** for CTE programs serving English learner populations
7. **Supplemental curriculum** that layers onto existing CTE coursework without replacing adopted programs
