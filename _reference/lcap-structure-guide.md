# California LCAP Structure Guide

## What Is an LCAP?

**LCAP** stands for **Local Control and Accountability Plan**. It is the primary planning document that every California school district, county office of education, and charter school must adopt to describe how they will serve students and spend funding.

LCAPs were mandated by the **Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF)**, signed into law in 2013 (AB 97, SB 91). LCFF replaced California's previous categorical funding system with a streamlined formula that gives districts more local control over spending, paired with accountability for outcomes.

## Plan Cycle

- LCAPs are **3-year plans**, updated annually.
- The current plan covers three fiscal years (e.g., 2024-25 through 2026-27).
- Each year, districts produce an **Annual Update** evaluating the prior year and revising goals, actions, and budgets for the years ahead.
- The LCAP must be **approved by the governing board by July 1** of each year.

## Annual Update Timeline

| Month | Activity |
|-------|----------|
| January-February | District begins reviewing data, surveying stakeholders |
| February-March | Stakeholder engagement meetings (parents, staff, students, community) |
| March-April | Draft LCAP revisions based on data and input |
| April-May | Advisory committee review (DELAC, PAC, SELPA as applicable) |
| May-June | Public hearing at board meeting (required before adoption) |
| June-July | Board adopts LCAP and budget by July 1 |
| July-August | County office of education reviews and approves |
| September+ | Implementation begins for new fiscal year |

## LCFF Funding Sources

### Base Grant
The foundational per-pupil funding every district receives. Adjusted by grade span:
- K-3: Highest base rate (includes class size reduction add-on)
- 4-6: Standard base rate
- 7-8: Slightly higher than 4-6
- 9-12: Highest overall rate

### Supplemental Grant
An additional **20%** of the adjusted base grant for each **unduplicated pupil**. Unduplicated pupils are students who are:
- **Low-income** (free/reduced-price meals eligible)
- **English learners** (EL)
- **Foster youth**

A student qualifying under multiple categories is counted only once (hence "unduplicated").

### Concentration Grant
An additional **65%** of the adjusted base grant for each unduplicated pupil **above 55%** of total enrollment. Districts with high concentrations of high-needs students receive this on top of supplemental funding.

**Key Rule**: Supplemental and Concentration grant funds must be used to **increase or improve services** for unduplicated pupils. This is a legal requirement and districts must demonstrate proportionality in their LCAP.

### Federal Funding (Common Sources in LCAPs)
| Source | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Title I, Part A** | Academic achievement for disadvantaged students |
| **Title II, Part A** | Teacher quality and professional development |
| **Title III** | English learner and immigrant student programs |
| **Title IV, Part A** | Student support and academic enrichment (including STEM, technology, well-rounded education) |
| **IDEA** | Special education services |

### Other State/Federal Funding Often Referenced
- **Educator Effectiveness Block Grant (EEBG)**
- **California Career Technical Education Incentive Grant (CTEIG)**
- **Expanded Learning Opportunities Program (ELO-P)**
- **After School Education and Safety (ASES)**
- **Arts, Music, and Instructional Materials Discretionary Block Grant**
- **Learning Recovery Emergency Block Grant**

## LCAP Document Structure

### 1. Plan Summary
High-level overview including:
- **General Information**: District name, contact, fiscal year, adoption date
- **Reflections on Prior Year**: What worked, what didn't
- **LCAP Highlights**: Major themes and priorities
- **Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI)**: If any schools are identified for federal intervention
- **Schools and Student Groups**: Demographic overview

### 2. Goals and Actions
This is the core of the LCAP. Each **Goal** includes:
- **Goal description** and rationale
- **Measuring and Reporting Results**: Metrics, baselines, and targets tied to state priorities
- **Actions**: Specific programs, services, or strategies to achieve the goal
- **Expenditures**: Budgeted amounts per action, broken down by funding source

A typical LCAP has 3-7 goals. Common goal themes:
1. Academic achievement / college and career readiness
2. Safe, healthy, and engaging learning environments
3. Parent and family engagement
4. English learner proficiency and reclassification
5. Staff recruitment, retention, and professional development

### 3. Increased or Improved Services for Unduplicated Pupils
This section is legally required and must demonstrate:
- The **estimated percentage** of supplemental/concentration funds
- How services for unduplicated pupils are **increased or improved** compared to services for all students
- Justification for any **LEA-wide** or **schoolwide** actions funded by supplemental/concentration grants

### 4. Action Tables
The budget tables at the end of the LCAP provide the most concrete financial detail.

## Action Tables Breakdown

### Table 1: Total Planned Expenditures
Lists every action across all goals with:
- Action number and description
- Total budgeted amount
- Whether the action contributes to increased/improved services for unduplicated pupils

### Table 2: Total Planned Expenditures by Funding Source
Breaks down expenditures by:
- LCFF Base
- LCFF Supplemental
- LCFF Concentration
- Federal funds (by title/program)
- Other state funds
- Local funds

### Table 3: Contributing Actions
Filters to show only actions that are designated as **contributing** to increased/improved services for unduplicated pupils. Includes:
- Planned expenditures for contributing actions
- Whether the action is LEA-wide, schoolwide, or limited to unduplicated pupils

### Table 4: Annual Update - Contributing Actions
Prior year actual expenditures compared to estimates for contributing actions. Shows whether the district spent what it planned.

### Table 5: Annual Update - LCFF Carryover
Tracks any unspent supplemental/concentration funds that must be carried forward. California requires districts to spend or encumber these funds; persistent carryover signals underspending on high-needs students.

## How to Read Budget Tables

When analyzing LCAP budget tables, look for:

1. **Action descriptions**: Read what the action actually funds (personnel, materials, contracts, technology).
2. **Funding source column**: Identify whether it's LCFF base vs. supplemental/concentration vs. federal. Supplemental/Concentration-funded actions must serve unduplicated pupils.
3. **Contributing column**: "Yes" means the district claims this action serves unduplicated pupils. Watch for vague LEA-wide actions marked as contributing -- these are sometimes challenged.
4. **Planned vs. actual**: In the Annual Update tables, large discrepancies between planned and actual spending indicate implementation problems.
5. **Personnel costs**: Many LCAP actions are primarily staffing (teachers, counselors, coaches). Look for the FTE or position details in the description.

## Where STEM Spending Typically Appears

STEM-related expenditures in LCAPs are rarely consolidated under a single goal or action. They are distributed across multiple areas:

### Academic Achievement Goals
- Curriculum adoption and instructional materials (science textbooks, math intervention programs)
- NGSS implementation support
- Math and science intervention programs (iReady, tutoring)
- AP/IB science and math course expansion

### CTE and College/Career Readiness Goals
- CTE pathway development (biotech, engineering, health science)
- Industry certifications and equipment
- Work-based learning coordination
- PLTW or other structured STEM programs

### Technology Goals
- 1:1 device programs
- Digital literacy and computer science courses
- Robotics and coding programs
- Technology infrastructure

### Professional Development Actions
- Science coaching positions
- NGSS training and conferences
- Math coaching and PLC time
- STEM-focused PD contracts

### Equity/Access Actions
- STEM programs targeted at unduplicated pupils
- After-school STEM enrichment (often ELO-P or ASES funded)
- Bilingual STEM instruction
- MESA (Mathematics, Engineering, Science Achievement) programs

### Title IV, Part A
This federal funding source explicitly supports STEM and technology. Look for Title IV in the funding source column -- it is often used for:
- STEM materials and supplies
- Technology purchases
- Well-rounded education programs

### Common Budget Line Items for STEM
- Instructional materials and supplies (4000s object codes)
- Contracted services for PD or curriculum (5000s object codes)
- Certificated salaries for STEM teachers/coaches (1000s object codes)
- Technology equipment (4000s or 6000s object codes)

## Tips for LCAP Analysis

1. **Search for keywords**: Use the keyword scan list (`lcap-stem-keywords.md`) to systematically find STEM-related content.
2. **Follow the money**: Action tables reveal actual dollar commitments behind narrative claims.
3. **Check contributing status**: Actions funded by supplemental/concentration grants serve unduplicated pupils -- a key equity signal.
4. **Compare years**: The Annual Update reveals whether districts follow through on plans.
5. **Note what's missing**: Districts that don't mention science, NGSS, or STEM in their LCAP may represent greenfield opportunities for ModelIt.
6. **Read the metrics**: Goals tied to CAASPP math scores, CAST science scores, or CTE pathway completion rates signal STEM investment priorities.
