# ModelIt Value Propositions Mapped to LCAP Priorities

## Overview

This document maps ModelIt's capabilities to the most common priorities found in California district LCAPs. Use this as a reference when analyzing a district's LCAP to identify alignment points and craft tailored outreach messaging.

---

## 1. Math Proficiency Gap

**LCAP Priority**: Improving CAASPP math scores, closing achievement gaps, math intervention

**The Problem**: California districts consistently report that students perform below grade level in mathematics. CAASPP math proficiency rates remain stubbornly low, especially for socioeconomically disadvantaged students, English learners, and students with disabilities.

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> Computational modeling builds mathematical reasoning by giving students a context for applying algebraic thinking, proportional reasoning, and data analysis in authentic problem-solving scenarios.

**Key Talking Points**:
- Mathematical modeling is explicitly called out in the Common Core State Standards for Mathematics (CCSS-M) Standards for Mathematical Practice (SMP.4: Model with mathematics)
- Students use variables, equations, rates, and graphs within the modeling environment -- reinforcing abstract math concepts through concrete application
- Low-floor, high-ceiling design means students at different math levels can engage with the same modeling activity
- Built-in data visualization (graphs, tables) helps students connect mathematical representations to real-world phenomena
- Research shows that applied, context-rich math instruction improves both engagement and achievement

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `math proficiency`, `CAASPP math`, `math intervention`, `mathematical modeling`, `achievement gap`, `distance from standard`

## 2. NGSS Alignment

**LCAP Priority**: Implementing Next Generation Science Standards, adopting NGSS-aligned curriculum, CAST performance

**The Problem**: California adopted NGSS in 2013, but many districts are still in the early stages of full NGSS implementation. Science instruction often remains textbook-driven rather than phenomenon-based and three-dimensional.

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> "Developing and Using Models" is one of the eight Science and Engineering Practices (SEPs) at the heart of NGSS. ModelIt provides students with a computational tool to build, test, and refine models -- directly addressing this core practice.

**Key Talking Points**:
- NGSS SEP #2: Developing and Using Models is a progression from K-12, with increasing sophistication at each grade band
- ModelIt supports three-dimensional learning by integrating modeling (SEP) with disciplinary core ideas (DCI) and crosscutting concepts (CCC) like Systems and System Models, Cause and Effect, and Patterns
- NGSS performance expectations in life science, earth science, and physical science all include modeling tasks
- CAST (California Science Test) assesses students on their ability to use models to explain phenomena -- ModelIt provides practice in this exact skill
- Supplements existing NGSS curricula (Amplify, FOSS, OpenSciEd, STEMscopes) rather than replacing them

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `NGSS`, `Next Generation Science Standards`, `CAST`, `phenomenon-based`, `three-dimensional`, `science and engineering practices`, `curriculum adoption`

## 3. Equity and Access

**LCAP Priority**: Serving unduplicated pupils, English learner support, closing opportunity gaps

**The Problem**: Supplemental and Concentration grant funds must be used to increase or improve services for unduplicated pupils (low-income, English learners, foster youth). Districts need programs that demonstrably serve these populations.

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> ModelIt provides equitable access to computational STEM learning through bilingual support (English/Spanish), low-floor/high-ceiling activities that meet students where they are, and visual/interactive interfaces that reduce language barriers.

**Key Talking Points**:
- **Bilingual EN/ES interface**: Native Spanish-language support serves English learners and newcomer students without requiring translation workarounds
- **Low-floor, high-ceiling design**: Students can begin with simple models and progress to complex systems -- no prerequisite math or CS knowledge required
- **Visual, interactive learning**: Model building uses drag-and-drop, visual representations, and dynamic graphs that reduce reliance on English text comprehension
- **Culturally relevant phenomena**: Modeling activities can be contextualized to students' communities (local ecosystems, health issues, environmental justice)
- **Addresses opportunity gap**: Many Title I and high-poverty schools lack access to computational STEM tools; ModelIt brings NSF-quality tools to underserved classrooms
- **Qualifies as increased/improved services**: Can be specifically targeted to schools or student groups with high unduplicated pupil counts

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `unduplicated pupils`, `English learners`, `equity`, `opportunity gap`, `achievement gap`, `socioeconomically disadvantaged`, `bilingual`, `culturally responsive`

## 4. College and Career Readiness

**LCAP Priority**: Improving CCI scores, A-G completion, preparing students for post-secondary success

**The Problem**: The College/Career Indicator (CCI) on the California Dashboard measures whether students graduate "Prepared" for college and career. Districts are actively seeking programs that move students from "Not Prepared" to "Prepared."

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> Computational modeling is a real-world skill used across STEM careers -- from epidemiology to climate science to engineering design. ModelIt gives students hands-on experience with the type of systems thinking and data reasoning that colleges and employers value.

**Key Talking Points**:
- Modeling and simulation are used in virtually every STEM profession: public health (disease modeling), environmental science (ecosystem modeling), engineering (systems modeling), business (economic modeling)
- Develops 21st-century skills: critical thinking, computational thinking, data literacy, iterative design
- Supports A-G science coursework by providing rigorous, applied learning experiences
- Can be integrated into AP Environmental Science, AP Biology, AP Computer Science Principles
- Dual enrollment partnerships: ModelIt activities align with community college STEM course expectations
- Portfolio-worthy student projects demonstrate college and career readiness

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `college readiness`, `career readiness`, `CCI`, `A-G`, `future ready`, `workforce development`, `21st century skills`

## 5. CTE Enhancement

**LCAP Priority**: Developing CTE pathways, increasing CTE concentrators/completers, industry-aligned skills

**The Problem**: Districts are investing heavily in CTE pathways (often funded by CTEIG, Perkins V, or Strong Workforce), but need curriculum tools that bridge academic content and career skills, especially in STEM-related sectors.

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> ModelIt enhances CTE pathways in biotechnology, environmental science, health science, and engineering by providing computational modeling tools that develop career-relevant systems thinking skills.

**Key Talking Points**:
- **Biotechnology pathway**: Model biological systems, population dynamics, enzyme kinetics, disease spread
- **Environmental science pathway**: Model ecosystems, pollution dispersion, resource management, climate variables
- **Health science pathway**: Model epidemiological data, public health interventions, physiological systems
- **Engineering pathway**: Model system behaviors, design iterations, input/output relationships
- Cross-pathway tool: A single platform serves multiple CTE sectors, maximizing ROI
- Supports CTE capstone projects with authentic modeling tasks
- Develops industry-relevant computational skills alongside domain knowledge

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `CTE`, `career technical education`, `biotech`, `biotechnology`, `health science`, `engineering`, `pathways`, `CTEIG`, `Perkins`, `work-based learning`

## 6. Environmental Literacy

**LCAP Priority**: Environmental education, sustainability, STEM CARES, climate literacy

**The Problem**: California has increasing emphasis on environmental literacy through initiatives like the Environmental Literacy Initiative, NGSS earth and life science standards, and district sustainability commitments. Many districts reference environmental education in their LCAPs but lack computational tools to make it rigorous.

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> ModelIt enables STEM CARES-type environmental modeling where students investigate real-world environmental phenomena -- ecosystems, pollution, climate, and resource management -- through computational simulation.

**Key Talking Points**:
- Model ecosystem dynamics: predator-prey, food webs, population growth, carrying capacity
- Model human impact: pollution spread, deforestation, urbanization effects, resource depletion
- Model climate variables: greenhouse gas accumulation, temperature change, sea level rise
- Connects environmental literacy to NGSS performance expectations in Earth and Space Sciences and Life Sciences
- Supports place-based learning: students can model their local environment (watershed, air quality, biodiversity)
- Aligns with California's Environmental Principles and Concepts (EP&Cs)
- Supports school garden, outdoor education, and sustainability programs with data-driven modeling components

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `environmental`, `sustainability`, `climate`, `ecology`, `environmental literacy`, `outdoor education`, `STEM CARES`

## 7. Data-Driven Instruction

**LCAP Priority**: Using data to inform instruction, diagnostic assessments, progress monitoring

**The Problem**: Districts invest heavily in diagnostic and benchmark assessment tools (iReady, MAP, CAASPP interim) but often lack tools that generate formative data within STEM instruction itself.

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> ModelIt includes built-in analytics that show student modeling progress, giving teachers real-time formative data on student understanding of systems, variables, relationships, and scientific reasoning.

**Key Talking Points**:
- Teacher dashboard shows which students have completed models, the complexity of their models, and their reasoning
- Formative assessment embedded in the learning activity -- no separate test needed
- Data on student model construction reveals misconceptions (e.g., confusing correlation with causation, missing feedback loops)
- Supports MTSS/RTI Tier 1 differentiation: teachers can identify students who need additional support based on modeling progress
- Complements (not replaces) existing assessment tools like iReady and MAP
- Aligns with district data-driven instruction initiatives and PLC data cycles

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `data-driven`, `formative`, `diagnostic`, `progress monitoring`, `benchmark`, `MTSS`, `RTI`, `PLC`

## 8. Professional Development

**LCAP Priority**: Teacher training, NGSS implementation PD, instructional coaching

**The Problem**: NGSS implementation is stalled in many districts because teachers lack training on three-dimensional instruction, particularly around the Science and Engineering Practices. Districts budget significant LCAP funds for science PD.

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> ModelIt includes comprehensive teacher training as part of implementation, with professional development designed to build teacher capacity in computational modeling, NGSS Science and Engineering Practices, and data-driven science instruction.

**Key Talking Points**:
- PD included with ModelIt adoption -- not an additional cost
- Training covers both the tool and the pedagogy: how to facilitate model-based instruction
- Supports NGSS SEP #2 (Developing and Using Models) implementation, the practice many teachers find hardest to teach
- Can be embedded in existing PD structures (PLC time, coaching cycles, release days)
- NSF SBIR-funded research has informed the PD design -- evidence-based approach
- Train-the-trainer model available for scaling within a district
- Ongoing support and coaching available beyond initial training

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `professional development`, `PD`, `teacher training`, `NGSS rollout`, `NGSS implementation`, `science coaching`, `instructional coach`, `TOSA`

## 9. Cost-Effectiveness

**LCAP Priority**: Maximizing impact per dollar, supplementing existing programs, efficient resource allocation

**The Problem**: Districts have already invested in core curriculum adoptions (Amplify, FOSS, Discovery Education, etc.) and cannot afford to replace them. They need supplemental tools that add value without duplicating existing investments.

**ModelIt Value Proposition**:
> ModelIt supplements existing curriculum rather than replacing it. It layers computational modeling onto whatever science and math curriculum a district has already adopted, extending instructional depth without requiring a full curriculum overhaul.

**Key Talking Points**:
- **Supplemental, not replacement**: Works alongside Amplify, FOSS, Discovery Ed, OpenSciEd, STEMscopes, Mystery Science, and other adopted curricula
- **Single platform, multiple subjects**: One tool serves science, math, CTE, and environmental education -- reducing per-subject tool costs
- **Low infrastructure requirements**: Web-based, runs on existing Chromebooks and devices; no special hardware needed
- **PD included**: No separate PD contract required
- **Scales efficiently**: Per-student or site licensing models that work within LCAP budgets
- **Multiple funding source eligibility**: Can be funded from LCFF Supplemental/Concentration, Title I, Title IV, CTEIG, Perkins, or EEBG depending on how it is deployed
- **Research-backed ROI**: NSF SBIR Phase II funding validates the tool's efficacy and design

**LCAP Keywords to Match**: `supplemental`, `cost-effective`, `instructional materials`, `curriculum`, `Title IV`, `Title I`
