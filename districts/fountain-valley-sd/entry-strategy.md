## The Hook

> "Fountain Valley is Orange County's highest-performing elementary district — 74% math proficiency, 8 Gold Ribbon Schools, robotics at every campus. But when you look closer, Hispanic students are at 57% math and English Learners at 43%. Math proficiency drops from 85% in 3rd grade to 68% in middle school. ModelIt is an NSF-funded computational modeling platform that deepens NGSS Practice 2 for your top performers while closing those equity gaps. Your students already use Amplify and Discovery Education for content — ModelIt lets them BUILD the models. It's the missing piece in three-dimensional science, and it's bilingual."

## Decision Process

In a well-structured suburban district, the curriculum adoption process follows a clear path:

1. **Director of Ed Services evaluates** → Dr. Spiratos identifies ModelIt as NGSS enrichment tool and presents to Dr. Gargus
2. **Asst. Superintendent of Ed Services recommends** → Dr. Gargus approves the tool and recommends to superintendent
3. **Superintendent approves budget** → Dr. Stopp signs off on the purchase (likely within her authority for supplemental tools)
4. **Business Services confirms funding** → Isidro Guerra (Asst. Supt. Business) identifies budget line
5. **Board approval** → Only if purchase exceeds district threshold (typically $50K+)
6. **Implementation** → Ed Services coordinates with site principals and teachers

**For a pilot (<$15K)**: Steps 1-4 only. Dr. Gargus and Dr. Stopp can likely approve without board action.
**For district-wide ($30-50K+)**: Full process including board approval.

## Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| "We're already high-performing — we don't need more tools" | HIGH | HIGH | Lead with NGSS depth and equity gaps. "74% is excellent — but 57% for Hispanic students isn't. And 68% in middle school math means 32% are falling behind." |
| Amplify perceived as covering modeling | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | "Amplify delivers phenomena-driven content with pre-built sims. ModelIt lets students BUILD models — fundamentally different cognitive work. It's Practice 2 depth that Amplify doesn't offer." |
| Budget consumed by Measure O | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Position ModelIt as instructional (General Fund/S&C), not facilities (Measure O). Separate budget categories. |
| Ed Services team too busy for new tools | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Offer turnkey implementation: PD, curriculum integration guides, ongoing support. Minimize adoption burden. |
| Teacher resistance (already using many tools) | LOW-MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Show integration with existing tools (Amplify, Chromebooks, Google Classroom). Not another silo — an enrichment layer. |
| District decides to wait for next adoption cycle | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | ModelIt is supplemental, not adoptable curriculum. No adoption cycle dependency. Can purchase anytime. |
| Competition from free tools (PhET) | LOW | LOW | "PhET lets students INTERACT with pre-built models. ModelIt lets students BUILD models — different cognition." |
