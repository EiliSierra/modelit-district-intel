## Why This District — ModelIt Fit

1. **Catastrophic math proficiency** — 13.29% overall; Grade 7 worst at 8.47% (vs. 34% state avg). Every grade band is in crisis.
2. **Science proficiency equally dire** — 11.37% CAST; EL students at only 3.28%. Massive "Nearly Met" cohort waiting for the right intervention.
3. **PLTW already in place at Junior High** — Mac Robertson teaches Design & Modeling (7th) and Automation & Robotics / Medical Detectives (8th). STEM culture exists; ModelIt extends it at the classroom level.
4. **No confirmed core science publisher** — District appears to have a science curriculum gap. ModelIt can anchor NGSS SEP-2 implementation without displacing existing tools.
5. **EL population understated** — CDE profile shows 249 EL students (12.8%), not the 1.6% in baseline data. Bilingual platform is a real differentiator.
6. **ASES after-school program** + Boys & Girls Club partnership — after-school STEM delivery vehicle for ModelIt pilot.
7. **Technology-ready** — Clever Portal + PowerSchool + MyTechDesk = existing digital infrastructure. ModelIt integrates via Clever with no new login.
8. **CEP (Community Eligibility Provision) participation** — district qualifies for free meals for all students, signaling higher poverty than SED baseline suggests. Title I and LCFF Supplemental funding available.
9. **Long-tenured superintendent (14+ years)** — Paul Miller is deeply embedded. Stable leadership = predictable multi-year budget cycles. A pilot approved once tends to renew.
10. **LCAP 2025-26 budgets $9.3M** for high-needs student services — room for supplemental EdTech that serves SED/EL populations.
