## Key Talking Points

1. **The PLTW-ModelIt Bridge**: "Your PLTW students learn to design systems. ModelIt is what comes next — they BUILD models of how those systems behave. Design and Modeling (7th grade PLTW) hands students the tools. ModelIt teaches them the science that makes the tools matter."

2. **The CAST Gap**: "Beardsley's CAST score is 11.37% overall and 3.28% for English Learners. That's not a student problem — it's a missing tool. Students who build their own computational models understand science at a deeper level. That's what we provide."

3. **The Bilingual Differentiator**: "249 of your students are English Learners. ModelIt is fully bilingual English/Spanish — every label, every instruction, every model. Your EL students interact with the same rigorous modeling tool at the same cognitive level as their English-speaking peers."

4. **The Clever Pitch**: "No new login. Students open Clever — which they already use — click ModelIt, and they're in their workspace. No password reset emails to Ryan Frank. No onboarding friction. Zero IT lift."

5. **The Funding Pitch**: "This is a Title IV-A (SSAE) purchase — Well-Rounded Educational Opportunities. That's the same fund you use for PLTW equipment and other STEM tools. Your LCFF Supplemental ($9.3M projected for 2025-26) can fund the EL-targeted component. Budget-neutral to your site budget."

6. **The NSF Credibility**: "ModelIt is NSF SBIR Phase II funded — that's independent research validation that the platform has scientific merit. This isn't a startup chasing a grant. This is peer-reviewed technology."

7. **The Pilot Framing**: "Start small: Grade 8 science, BJHS, one semester. We'll measure before and after. If CAST proxies don't improve, we walk away. If they do, you have evidence for your 2026 LCAP annual update and a clear case for district-wide expansion."

8. **The Kern County Peer**: "Arvin Union — your neighbor in Kern County — has the same CAST profile. When we show districts in your region what a pilot looks like, the data is compelling. We're building a Kern County STEM network."

9. **The Urgency**: "Three board seats are up for election in November 2026. Showing measurable academic progress before August 2026 gives your board a win before campaign season."

10. **The Student Engagement**: "Students who use ModelIt say it's the first time math feels like it does something real. Teachers say it's the first tool where students compete to improve their model, not just submit and forget."

| "We already have PLTW for STEM." | "PLTW teaches engineering design — ModelIt teaches computational modeling of SCIENTIFIC systems. They're complementary, not competing. Your PLTW students are the BEST candidates for ModelIt because they already think in systems. Mac Robertson would agree — Design & Modeling students are primed for this." |
| "Our test scores are low — we need to focus on basics first." | "Computational modeling IS the basics in science. NGSS SEP-2 is literally 'Developing and Using Models.' | "We need to see results before we commit." | "That's exactly why we offer a 6-week pilot. We'll define success metrics together — CAST-proxy scores, student engagement, teacher adoption. If we don't hit them, you don't renew. We're confident enough in the data to offer that." |

| "Our superintendent has been here 14 years — he's cautious about new programs." | "Paul Miller has been leading Beardsley since 2012. In that time, CAST science has stayed below 15%.
