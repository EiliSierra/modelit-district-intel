## The Hook

> "La Habra City Schools has built a strong STEM foundation — Science & Technology Academies at Walnut and Imperial, PLTW Gateway at your middle schools, 1:1 devices in every classroom, and now $73M through Measure O to modernize your science labs. But when those new labs open in 2026-27, what computational tools will your students use in them? With 86% Hispanic enrollment, 21% English Learners, and EL math proficiency at just 11.78%, your students need tools that make math meaningful — in both English and Spanish. ModelIt is an NSF-funded computational modeling platform that lets students BUILD models of real-world systems. It's the software that turns your new science labs and STEAM academies into computational thinking centers. It complements PLTW, runs on your existing Chromebooks and iPads, and is fully bilingual. Your LCAP emphasizes mathematics instruction and student discourse — computational modeling IS mathematical discourse in action."

## Decision Process

In a medium-sized K-8 district, the decision process follows standard channels:

1. **Asst. Superintendent of Ed Services evaluates** → Dr. Sandoval identifies ModelIt as LCAP-aligned math/science intervention
2. **Director of Special Programs confirms funding** → Emily Flesher identifies budget line (Title I, LCFF Supp/Conc, Title III)
3. **Superintendent approves** → Dr. Carlos signs off on tool and budget
4. **Pilot at STEAM Academy sites** → Imperial MS and Walnut Elementary as initial schools
5. **Board approval** → If purchase exceeds district threshold (likely >$50K requires board action)
6. **Implementation** → Science/math teachers at pilot sites, supported by Academic Coach

**Note**: Measure O bond purchases may require separate board approval through the bond oversight process.
