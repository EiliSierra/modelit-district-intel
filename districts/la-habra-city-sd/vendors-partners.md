## Vendor Landscape Analysis

### Where ModelIt Fits

| Position | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Direct competitors present** | PLTW is present but complementary (not competitive) |
| **Complementary tools** | Science & Technology Academies + PLTW → ModelIt adds computational modeling layer |
| **Gap ModelIt fills** | Computational modeling + systems thinking — almost certainly no current tool |
| **Price sensitivity** | MEDIUM — large budget ($82M revenue), Measure O bond, high federal/state funding |

### Warm Intro Paths

1. **PLTW connection**: If ModelIt has any relationship with PLTW or their partner network, this is a natural entry. The district already trusts PLTW for STEM education.

2. **Orange County Department of Education (OCDE)**: Dr. Carlos was profiled by OCDE Newsroom. County-level relationship could provide an introduction.

3. **Cal Poly Pomona**: Robotics competition connection. If ModelIt has university partners, this could be a warm path.

4. **Boys & Girls Club of La Habra**: If ModelIt can position within after-school STEM enrichment, this is a lower-barrier entry.

5. **Measure O advisory groups**: Bond oversight committees may welcome vendors who demonstrate value for new facilities.

### Competitive Threats

| Threat | Level | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Status quo (no new tools) | MEDIUM | District IS investing in STEM (PLTW, Academies, bond) but LCAP focus is on math instruction methods, not new tools |
| PLTW expansion consuming STEM budget | MEDIUM | If district expands PLTW to more grades/schools, budget may go there first |
| Free alternatives (PhET, etc.) | LOW | Free sims don't offer model-building |
| Other supplemental STEM vendors | LOW | No evidence of strong incumbent in computational modeling space |
| Budget constraints from Measure O debt service | LOW | Bond is separate from operational budget |
