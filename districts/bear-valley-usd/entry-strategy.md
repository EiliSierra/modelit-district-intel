## The Hook

> "Bear Valley has BEARTECH teaching nature-based science, a 213-acre outdoor education property, the Pebble Plain (7th most biodiverse place in the US), and a world-class solar observatory in your backyard. Your students observe these systems every day. But 70% of them still aren't meeting math standards — and at the high school, it's 82%. ModelIt bridges that gap. Your BEARTECH students can BUILD computational models of the ecosystems they study — population dynamics, water cycles, climate patterns. Your high school students can model solar physics using data from the observatory next door. It turns Big Bear's extraordinary natural environment into a mathematical laboratory. And with a board president who holds a PhD in Experimental Particle Physics, you have someone who understands computational modeling at the deepest possible level."

## Decision Process

Small unified district with an insider superintendent — decisions are relationship-driven:

1. **Executive Directors evaluate** → Leigh Anne Drake (TK-6) and Brian Willemse (6-12) assess ModelIt for their grade bands
2. **Superintendent endorses** → Manny Marquez supports the tool (24-year insider = community trust)
3. **Board President advocates** → Dr. Foulkes (PhD Physics) champions computational modeling at board level
4. **CBO confirms funding** → Budget officer identifies funding line (Title I, LCFF, Foundation)
5. **Board approval** → Only if purchase exceeds district threshold
6. **Implementation** → MS/HS science and math teachers, plus BEARTECH integration at elementary

**No RFP process** expected for a pilot. Superintendent and Executive Directors have authority for instructional material purchases in a district this size.
