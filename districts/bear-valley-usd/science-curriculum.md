## What This Means for ModelIt

### Positioning Strategy

ModelIt should be positioned as the **computational modeling layer** that transforms BEARTECH from a nature-observation program into a computational science program:

1. **BEARTECH + ModelIt**: BEARTECH teaches students to observe and interact with mountain ecosystems. ModelIt lets them BUILD computational models of those systems — predator-prey dynamics, forest fire spread, water cycles, climate patterns. This is the missing computational dimension.

2. **Mountain ecosystem laboratory**: Big Bear Lake, Pebble Plain (7th most biodiverse place in US), San Bernardino National Forest, 7,200 ft elevation — every NGSS domain has a local phenomenon ready for modeling.

3. **Solar Observatory connection**: The Big Bear Solar Observatory (operated by NJIT) is a world-class research facility. Students can model solar activity, radiation patterns, and astronomical systems using real data from their own community.

4. **213-acre living laboratory**: The Education Foundation donated 213 acres for outdoor education. ModelIt makes this property a computational science field site — students collect data outdoors and build models indoors.

5. **Board President understands modeling**: Dr. Foulkes (PhD Particle Physics) spent his career doing computational modeling. He can advocate for ModelIt from a position of deep personal expertise.
