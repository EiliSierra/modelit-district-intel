### Comparative Funding Position

| Metric | Bear Valley USD | State Median | Difference |
|--------|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| Per-Pupil Revenue | $17,659 | $19,974 | -$2,315 |
| Per-Pupil Expenditure | $15,678 | $18,395 | -$2,717 |

**Key Insight**: Bear Valley USD operates significantly below the state median on both revenue and expenditure per pupil. The 43% local funding share (from property taxes in a tourism/resort community) is relatively high but still insufficient to close the gap. This is a district that must be strategic about every dollar — position ModelIt through grant funding, subsidized pilots, or foundation partnerships rather than general fund purchases.

## Key LCAP Priorities

The superintendent's "Educate, Inspire, Prepare" framework signals the district is focused on:
1. **Improving academic outcomes** (30.35% math proficiency demands intervention)
2. **Student engagement through place-based learning** (BEARTECH program, outdoor education)
3. **Career readiness and CTE** (Career Center, dual enrollment, Real World Project)

ModelIt sits at the intersection of all three priorities — computational modeling is an academic intervention, an engaging place-based tool (mountain ecology models), and a career-relevant STEM skill.

## Budget Entry Points for ModelIt

| Funding Source | Est. Available | Rationale | Likelihood |
|---------------|---------------|-----------|-----------|
| **LCFF Supplemental/Concentration** | $50-200K | Math intervention for unduplicated pupils (43.1% Hispanic + 13.6% EL + 46.9% ED) | HIGH |
| **Title I** | $50-100K | CEP district, math intervention focus | HIGH |
| **Title IV-A (SSAE)** | $15-30K | STEM/well-rounded education allocation | MEDIUM |
| **Ed Trust / Foundation partnership** | $5-20K | BEARTECH expansion, STEMQUEST museum initiative | MEDIUM |
| **Title II-A** | $5-10K | PD component for ModelIt teacher training | MEDIUM |
| **DonorsChoose** | $1-5K per project | Teacher-initiated, 26 projects already funded | LOW (teacher-level) |

**Recommended Primary Pathway**: LCFF Supplemental + Title I co-funding for a math intervention pilot targeting grades 5-8 where the proficiency drop-off begins. Foundation partnership as supplementary funding for BEARTECH integration.

**Pricing Strategy**:
- ~2,132 students district-wide — moderate-size opportunity
- Pilot at BBMS (494 students) + BBHS (626 students) = ~1,120 secondary students at $8-15K
- Elementary BEARTECH add-on (Baldwin Lane + North Shore) = $5-8K
- District-wide license = $15-25K
- Position as grant-funded or foundation-supported given below-median funding
