## Key Talking Points

1. **"70% of your students aren't meeting math standards — and at high school it's 82%"**: "Math proficiency drops from 46% at elementary to 18% at high school. Something is breaking in the pipeline. ModelIt provides a different kind of math engagement — students build computational models of systems they can see and measure, making abstract math concrete and meaningful."

2. **"BEARTECH + ModelIt = complete science"**: "BEARTECH teaches students to observe nature. ModelIt teaches students to model nature. Observation is the first step of the scientific method — computation is the next. Together, they complete the process: observe, hypothesize, model, predict, test."

3. **"Big Bear is a laboratory"**: "Your students live at 6,750 feet in the San Bernardino National Forest, beside Big Bear Lake, near the Pebble Plain — the 7th most biodiverse place in the US. This isn't a field trip. This is daily life. ModelIt turns that daily life into mathematical inquiry."

4. **"Solar Observatory connection"**: "You have a world-class NJIT solar physics research facility in your community. ModelIt lets students model the same solar phenomena the scientists study. Imagine a high school student building a computational model of solar flares using context from the observatory next door."

5. **"Board President gets it"**: "Dr. Stephen Foulkes has a PhD in Experimental Particle Physics from UC Riverside. He doesn't just understand STEM education — he IS a computational scientist. When he reviews ModelIt, he'll recognize it immediately as the kind of tool that builds real scientific thinking."

6. **"Bilingual by design"**: "With 43% Hispanic students and 13.6% English Learners, language accessibility matters. ModelIt is fully bilingual — English and Spanish. Every student can build models in the language they think in."

7. **"Below-median funding, above-average assets"**: "Your per-pupil funding is $2,315 below state median. But your natural assets — the lake, the forest, the observatory, the Pebble Plain — are priceless. ModelIt is how you turn free natural phenomena into rigorous math instruction."

## Objection Handling

| # | Objection | Response |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | "BEARTECH already covers our science needs." | "BEARTECH is observation-based. ModelIt is computation-based. They're different cognitive activities. BEARTECH students observe a meadow ecosystem. ModelIt students BUILD a computational model of that ecosystem — predicting what happens when variables change. Together, they complete the scientific process." |
| 2 | "Our budget is too tight — we're below state median." | "Three paths: Title I (CEP district = strong eligibility), LCFF Supplemental (high unduplicated count), or Education Trust co-funding (they already fund BEARTECH). A subsidized pilot is $5-10K. We'll work with you on grant positioning." |
| 3 | "Our students struggle with basic math — modeling is too advanced." | "ModelIt IS how they learn math. Students who fail abstract math succeed when they see math working in a real system. Modeling population changes in a meadow ecosystem is multiplication and ratios in a context that makes sense to a Big Bear kid." |
| 4 | "We're isolated up here — vendors don't support us." | "We intentionally serve mountain and rural districts. NSF funded ModelIt to reach underserved students. We provide virtual support with in-person visits, and Big Bear's environment is literally our best sales demo." |
| 5 | "The superintendent is new — we're not ready for new tools." | "Manny Marquez may be new to the superintendent role, but he's a 24-year BVUSD veteran. He knows what this community needs. A small pilot is low-risk and gives him data for LCAP planning." |
| 6 | "We're focused on STEMQUEST (the museum initiative)." | "STEMQUEST is a physical museum. ModelIt is a digital platform. They're complementary — students visit the museum to interact with physical phenomena, then use ModelIt in class to build computational models of what they experienced. ModelIt could even be featured inside the museum." |
| 7 | "Free tools like PhET already exist." | "PhET lets students interact with pre-built simulations. ModelIt lets students BUILD their own models from scratch. That's a fundamentally different cognitive activity — creation vs. consumption. Building a model requires mathematical reasoning in a way that clicking through a simulation never will." |
