## The Hook

"Only 20.68% of EUHSD students meet math standards -- and for Hispanic students, it's just 14.88%. Your district has a dedicated Science Content Specialist and Engineering & Design CTE pathways, but students need a tool to BUILD computational models, not just learn about science. ModelIt is an NSF-funded, bilingual platform that lets students construct systems models aligned to NGSS Practice 2 -- and with just 5 schools, you could see district-wide impact within a single year."

## Decision Process

1. Content Specialist (Chamberlin) identifies need and evaluates tools
2. Recommends to Assistant Superintendent (Casas)
3. Pilot approved at 1-2 schools with teacher PD
4. Results reviewed by Educational Services team
5. Budget approved through LCAP or Title funding
6. Board consent calendar for purchase
7. District-wide rollout across 5 schools

## 5-Phase Approach

### Phase 1: Research & Connect (Weeks 1-3)
- Call Shannon Chamberlin at (760) 291-3021 to introduce ModelIt
- Being local (Carlsbad), offer an in-person demo at the district office
- Research any SDCOE STEM initiatives that include EUHSD
- Prepare Escondido-specific data one-pager

### Phase 2: Demo & Evaluate (Weeks 4-8)
- Schedule in-person demo at 302 N. Midway Drive
- Include Renee Farrell (math) and Vivian Carranco (CTE) in demo
- Provide teacher trial accounts for evaluation
- Connect ModelIt to their Engineering & Design CTE pathway

### Phase 3: Pilot Proposal (Weeks 9-12)
- Propose pilot at 2 schools (1 middle-grade focus, 1 CTE-focused)
- Align with LCAP goals for math/science improvement
- Include bilingual features pitch for 79% Hispanic enrollment
- Budget proposal mapped to Title IV-A or LCFF Supplemental

### Phase 4: Pilot Execution (Weeks 13-26)
- Launch pilot with teacher PD
- Regular check-ins with Shannon Chamberlin
- Collect student engagement and assessment data
- Present mid-pilot progress

### Phase 5: District Adoption (Weeks 27-40)
- Present pilot results to Martin Casas and Jon Petersen
- 5-school district = rapid scaling
- Full PD for all science and CTE teachers
- Board consent calendar approval

## Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Small budget ($151M revenue) | MEDIUM | HIGH | Start with smallest viable pilot; use Title IV-A |
| Science specialist resistant | LOW | VERY HIGH | She's the gatekeeper; invest in relationship first |
| Recent curriculum adoption | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Position as supplement, not replacement |
| Board resistance | LOW | MEDIUM | 5-school district = lower cost; build staff support first |
| Competing priorities (math crisis) | HIGH | MEDIUM | Frame ModelIt as math AND science tool (computational thinking) |
| Limited staff bandwidth | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Provide turnkey PD and support |
| Free alternatives preferred | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Emphasize student-built models vs pre-made simulations |
