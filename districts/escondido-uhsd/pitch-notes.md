| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We're focused on math, not science right now" | "ModelIt IS a math tool as much as a science tool. Computational modeling builds the quantitative reasoning and systems thinking that directly supports math proficiency. It addresses both gaps simultaneously." |
| "We're a small district with limited budget" | "That's actually an advantage. With 5 schools, a pilot costs a fraction of what larger districts spend. We can start with Title IV-A funding -- typically $20-50K -- and prove impact before scaling." |
| "Our teachers are already overloaded" | "ModelIt integrates into existing science and CTE courses. Our PD is hands-on and practical -- teachers leave ready to use it the next day. And we're local for ongoing support." |
| "We already have a science curriculum" | "ModelIt doesn't replace your curriculum -- it enhances it. Whatever textbook or program you use, ModelIt adds the student-built modeling dimension that NGSS requires but most curricula don't provide." |
| "How is this different from PhET or Gizmos?" | "PhET and Gizmos are pre-built simulations students interact with. ModelIt lets students design and build their own models from scratch. It's the difference between reading about engineering and actually designing a system." |

| "Our board won't approve another purchase" | "Most ModelIt adoptions go through Title IV-A or LCFF supplemental -- both are at the superintendent/cabinet level, not requiring separate board action beyond consent calendar." |
