## Vendor Landscape Analysis

### Where ModelIt Fits

| Position | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Direct competitors present** | Unknown - likely minimal in a small rural district |
| **Complementary tools** | Zoo Safari Park (observation-based) → ModelIt adds computational modeling |
| **Gap ModelIt fills** | Computational modeling + systems thinking - likely no current tool for this |
| **Price sensitivity** | HIGH - small district, limited budget, every dollar scrutinized |

### Warm Intro Paths

1. **San Diego Zoo Safari Park**: If ModelIt has or could develop conservation/ecology models, the Zoo partnership creates a natural introduction. "Your students observe ecosystems at the Safari Park - now they can model those systems computationally."

2. **SP School Foundation**: Foundation explicitly funds technology and curriculum. A proposal to the Foundation Board could fund a ModelIt pilot independently of district budget.

3. **SDCOE**: San Diego County Office of Education may facilitate introductions to small districts seeking STEM tools.

### Competitive Threats

| Threat | Level | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Status quo (no new tools) | HIGH | Small district may prefer to keep things simple |
| Budget constraints | HIGH | ~$9M total revenue, limited discretionary spending |
| Free alternatives (PhET, etc.) | MEDIUM | Free simulations exist but don't offer model-building |
| Math adoption consuming budget | MEDIUM | New math purchase may exhaust instructional materials budget |
