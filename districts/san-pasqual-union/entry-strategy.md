## Decision Process

In a small single-school district, the decision process is dramatically simpler than in large districts:

1. **Superintendent/Principal decides** → Mark Burroughs evaluates the tool
2. **Teacher input** → STEM teachers confirm it fits their curriculum
3. **CBO confirms funding** → Kristin DiNofia identifies budget line
4. **Board approval** → Only if purchase exceeds district threshold (likely >$5-10K)
5. **Implementation** → 2-3 teachers in MS STEM/Science classes

**No RFP process** expected for a small pilot purchase. Superintendent has authority for smaller instructional material purchases.

## Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Burroughs says no | Medium | Critical | Lead with teacher enthusiasm first. If Lynch is excited, Burroughs is more likely to approve. |
| Budget too tight | Medium | High | Propose Foundation funding path. Saints Soirée explicitly funds tech/curriculum. |
| Math adoption consuming budget | Medium | Medium | Position ModelIt as complementary to new math, not competing for same dollars. |
| Small district = small deal | Certain | Low | Accept small initial deal ($3-5K). Value is in the testimonial and proximity for ongoing support. |
| Teacher turnover | Low | High | Train both STEM teachers. In a small school, turnover would be district-wide disruption regardless. |
| Tech infrastructure limitations | Low | Medium | ModelIt is web-based. Check device:student ratio at visit. |
