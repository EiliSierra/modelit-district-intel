## Outreach Priority Order

1. **Mark Burroughs** (Superintendent/Principal) - In a 548-student district, he IS the decision maker. No layers to navigate. Personal relationship approach - he's been here 20 years and knows every family.

2. **Jenny Lynch** (MS STEM Teacher) - Dedicated STEM teacher is the ideal classroom champion. She teaches the students who would use ModelIt. Get her excited first, then she advocates to Burroughs.

3. **Amy Cutchin** (MS STEM Teacher) - Second STEM teacher. Having both STEM teachers on board creates critical mass in a small school.

4. **Jolene Mallory** (MS Science Teacher) - Science teacher completes the middle school STEM team. Three teachers advocating together is powerful.

5. **Kristin DiNofia** (CBO/Director of Finance) - Knows which budget lines have room. Can identify Title IV-A or LCFF supplemental funds.

6. **Kami Goe** (Board President) - If Burroughs is supportive, board approval is likely. But having board president aware of the initiative helps.

## Key Relationships & Notes

- **Burroughs is the bottleneck AND the accelerator**: In a single-school district, if the superintendent/principal says yes, it happens. If he says no, there's no alternate path.
- **Teachers are accessible**: Small school means you can meet STEM teachers at the same visit as the superintendent.
- **Foundation & PTO are influential**: SP School Foundation and PTO raise significant supplemental funds. Saints Soirée (March 2026) explicitly funds technology and curriculum.
- **Community is tight-knit**: San Pasqual Valley is a rural community. Personal introductions matter more than cold emails.
