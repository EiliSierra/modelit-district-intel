## The Hook

"Sweetwater UHSD has 34,445 students, a dedicated STEAM department, and the SDSU Compact for Success -- yet only 30.44% of students meet math standards, and Hispanic students are at 23.21%. ModelIt is an NSF-funded, bilingual computational modeling platform that lets your STEAM students build their own systems models. It bridges the gap between your CTE pathways and the computational thinking skills SDSU expects -- and with 70% Hispanic enrollment, our full Spanish interface ensures every student can access the science."

## Decision Process

1. Assistant Superintendent Teaching & Learning (Alvarez) identifies need
2. STEAM department evaluates tool alignment
3. Pilot approved at 3-5 schools
4. Teacher PD and implementation
5. Results reviewed by Teaching & Learning team
6. Budget through LCAP or Title funding
7. Board consent calendar for purchase
8. Scale across 29 schools

## 5-Phase Approach

### Phase 1: Direct Outreach (Weeks 1-4)
- Email Ana Maria Alvarez at Ana.Alvarez@sweetwaterschools.org
- Separate equity-focused email to Vernon.Moore@sweetwaterschools.org
- Call STEAM department (ccr.sweetwaterschools.org) to identify director
- Prepare Sweetwater-specific data one-pager

### Phase 2: Demo & Evaluation (Weeks 5-10)
- Schedule demo at Sweetwater district office (1130 Fifth Ave, Chula Vista)
- Include STEAM department staff and CTE representatives
- Provide teacher trial accounts at 2-3 sites
- Connect ModelIt to SDSU Compact for Success pipeline

### Phase 3: Pilot Proposal (Weeks 11-16)
- Propose pilot at 4-5 schools (given 29-school size)
- Budget mapped to Title IV-A and LCFF Supplemental
- Include IB science enrichment angle
- PD plan for pilot teachers

### Phase 4: Pilot Execution (Weeks 17-34)
- Launch pilot with teacher training
- Regular check-ins with STEAM department
- Collect student engagement and assessment data
- Engage SDSU Compact team for college readiness angle

### Phase 5: District Adoption (Weeks 35-60)
- Present results to Alvarez and Aguirre
- Phased rollout across 29 schools (3 waves)
- Scale PD program
- Board consent calendar for full adoption

## Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| District too large/complex | HIGH | HIGH | Start with focused pilot (4-5 schools); phased rollout |
| Below-median per-pupil spending | HIGH | HIGH | Multiple funding sources; strong ROI argument |
| STEAM dept already has tools | MEDIUM | HIGH | Position as complement; fill modeling gap |
| Superintendent is ops-focused | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Primary path through Alvarez (Teaching & Learning) |
| Long procurement cycle | HIGH | MEDIUM | Start now for 2027-28 full adoption |
| PLTW or competitor entrenched | MEDIUM | HIGH | Differentiate: student-built models vs pre-made content |
| Too many competing programs | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Align with existing STEAM/CTE infrastructure |
| Board politics | LOW | MEDIUM | Build staff consensus first; board follows |
