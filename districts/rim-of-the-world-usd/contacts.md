## Key Relationships & Notes

- **Ridland is the golden contact**: A board member who is ALSO a former math teacher with an Ed Tech master's degree is extraordinarily rare. She understands the problem (math gap), the solution space (technology), and the approval process (board). Cultivate this relationship first.
- **Scott Craft is a CTE ally**: 33-year retired teacher/coach who advocates for CTE. If ModelIt connects to CTE pathways, Craft becomes a second board champion.
- **Loncteaux family**: Christopher Loncteaux is a TOSA, Jaclyn Loncteaux is a principal (Lake Arrowhead Elem). Family connection suggests deep community roots.
- **Foundation is an alternate budget path**: Rim Educational Foundation has funded CTE since 2015 and AVID since 2009. Annual school grants ($2-7K per school) could fund a ModelIt pilot without touching district budget.
- **Mountain community = word of mouth**: Small mountain towns mean everyone knows everyone. One positive impression ripples through the community quickly. One negative impression does the same.
- **New superintendent = window of opportunity**: Sevillano is still establishing his agenda (2 years in). He needs wins. ModelIt addressing the math crisis is a potential win.
