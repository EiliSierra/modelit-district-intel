## The Hook

> "Rim of the World has solar panels and wind turbines that make your campus a living energy lab. You have Fire Science students studying wildfire in a community that lives with fire risk every season. You have construction students building real structures. But 83% of your students still aren't meeting math standards. ModelIt bridges that gap — your students can BUILD computational models of the energy systems on your roof, the fire spread patterns in your forests, and the structural loads in their construction projects. It turns the mountain environment your students live in into a mathematical laboratory. With $71M in Measure E flowing into STEM facilities, ModelIt is the instructional software that makes those facilities more powerful. And with a former math teacher on your board who holds an MA in Ed Tech, you have a champion who already understands why this matters."

## Decision Process

Medium-sized unified district with relatively standard decision-making:

1. **Director of Ed Services evaluates** → Jennifer Whiteside identifies ModelIt as a math/science intervention
2. **CTE Coordinator assesses CTE fit** → Stephanie Phillips confirms Fire Science/Construction pathway alignment
3. **Superintendent approves** → Dr. Sevillano signs off on tool and budget
4. **CBO confirms funding** → Jenny Haberlin identifies budget line (LCFF, Title I, CTE)
5. **Board awareness** → Jordana Ridland (former math teacher) champions at board level
6. **Board approval** → If purchase exceeds district threshold (~$15K+)
7. **Implementation** → MS/HS science, math, and CTE teachers

**No RFP process** expected for a pilot-scale purchase. Superintendent and Director of Educational Services have authority for instructional material purchases under threshold.
