## Key Talking Points

1. **"83% of your students aren't meeting math standards"**: "That's not a gap — it's a crisis. Traditional interventions aren't closing it. ModelIt gives students a fundamentally different way to engage with math — they build computational models of systems they can see, touch, and measure."

2. **"Your campus IS the curriculum"**: "You're the first school in California with both solar panels AND wind turbines. Your students live inside the San Bernardino National Forest. ModelIt turns these assets into math lessons — students model energy output, forest ecosystems, fire behavior, and water cycles using real data from their own environment."

3. **"Measure E makes ModelIt more powerful"**: "$71M is upgrading your STEM facilities. ModelIt is the software that runs inside those facilities. Without computational tools, you have great buildings. With ModelIt, you have great buildings AND great instruction."

4. **"Middle school is the crisis point"**: "Math proficiency drops to ~12% at Mary Henck Intermediate. By the time students reach Rim High, many have given up on math. ModelIt can intervene at middle school with engaging, visual, hands-on computational models that rebuild math confidence."

5. **"Fire Science + ModelIt"**: "Your Fire Science students study wildfire in a community where fire is existential. ModelIt lets them model fire spread — terrain, wind, humidity, fuel load. It's the computational science behind firefighting, and it makes math relevant to students who want careers in public safety."

6. **"Bilingual by design"**: "With 44% Hispanic students and 9% English Learners, language shouldn't block access to computational thinking. ModelIt is fully bilingual — every student can build models in English or Spanish."

7. **"Board champion ready"**: "Jordana Ridland is a former math teacher with an MA in Educational Technology. She understands both the math crisis and the technology solution space. You already have a board-level advocate."

## Objection Handling

| # | Objection | Response |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | "We're focused on Measure E facilities right now — instructional tools are next year." | "Measure E is capital budget; ModelIt is instructional budget — different funding streams. You can start a pilot now with LCFF/Title I while facilities are being upgraded. When the new STEM spaces open, ModelIt is already in teachers' hands." |
| 2 | "Our students struggle with basic math — they're not ready for modeling." | "ModelIt IS how they learn math. Students who fail abstract math often succeed when they can see the math working. Modeling energy from your own solar panels uses multiplication, ratios, and graphs — in a context that makes sense." |
| 3 | "We're a mountain community — most vendors forget about us." | "We serve rural and mountain districts intentionally. NSF funded this to reach underserved students. We provide virtual support with in-person visits, and your mountain environment is literally our best demo — the forest, the lakes, the fire ecology." |
| 4 | "Budget is tight beyond Measure E." | "Four funding paths: LCFF Supplemental ($5.3M projected), Title I (39.6% ED), CTE grants (Fire Science + Construction), or Rim Educational Foundation. A pilot is $10-15K — a fraction of your instructional budget." |
| 5 | "We already have AVID for college readiness." | "AVID is organizational skills and study habits. ModelIt is computational thinking — a different cognitive skill set. Together, AVID + ModelIt = students who are both organized AND analytically capable. They're complementary, not competing." |
| 6 | "Our CTE is trades-focused — not really a math tool." | "Every trade benefits from modeling. Fire Science students model fire behavior. Construction students model structural loads and energy efficiency. Automotive students model engine systems. ModelIt makes CTE more rigorous without losing the hands-on identity." |
| 7 | "We've tried tech tools before and they didn't stick." | "Most tech tools are consumption-based — students click through content. ModelIt is creation-based — students build things. That's fundamentally different engagement. And at your size, we provide personalized support to ensure teachers succeed." |
