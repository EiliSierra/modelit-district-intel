## What This Means for ModelIt

### Positioning Strategy

ModelIt should be positioned as a **computational modeling platform** that transforms Rim's unique mountain ecosystem and STEM infrastructure into data-driven learning experiences:

1. **Mountain ecosystem connection**: Students live IN a natural laboratory - ModelIt lets them BUILD computational models of the ecosystems surrounding them (forest growth, fire spread, watershed dynamics, wildlife populations, weather patterns)
2. **Renewable energy connection**: Students already study solar and wind energy on campus - ModelIt lets them model energy production, efficiency variables, and grid integration
3. **Fire Science CTE connection**: Fire Science pathway students can model fire spread patterns, terrain effects, weather influences, and forest management strategies
4. **Math crisis intervention**: With 17-19% math proficiency, ModelIt applies mathematical reasoning to real-world mountain systems students care about
5. **FFA/Environmental connection**: FFA students studying forestry can model forest growth, logging sustainability, replanting strategies, and ecosystem recovery

### How ModelIt Complements Current Tools

| Current Tool/Program | What It Does | What ModelIt Adds |
|---------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Renewable Energy Project (Solar + Wind) | Students observe real-time energy production | Students model energy systems, predict output, optimize variables |
| FFA Forestry/Environmental | Hands-on forestry and ecology work | Computational models of forest ecosystems, fire ecology, watershed dynamics |
| Fire Science CTE | Fire behavior theory and practice | Data-driven fire spread models, terrain/weather/fuel modeling |
| Robotics Team | Build and program robots | Systems modeling for engineering design and optimization |
| AVID | Academic rigor and study skills | Structured computational thinking activities aligned to college readiness |
| Mountain Ecosystem (K-12) | Living in the San Bernardino National Forest | Model the ecosystem students walk through every day - make it computational |
