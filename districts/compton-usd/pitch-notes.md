## Email Template: Primary Contact (Jorge Torres)

**Subject**: Computational Modeling for Compton USD's STEM Pathways -- NSF-Funded Platform

Dear Dr. Torres,

Congratulations on Compton USD's remarkable achievement -- #1 in California for test score gains. The momentum your team has built in ELA and math is extraordinary.

I'm reaching out because I believe the next frontier for CUSD is science proficiency. With math now at 41% (above state average) and ELA at 51%, the district's data-driven approach has proven it works. Yet science remains at approximately 14% -- about half the state average. Your existing STEM programs (robotics, coding, NAF Academy pathways in Aerospace and Biotechnology) have built the infrastructure. What's missing is a tool that lets students BUILD their own computational models of scientific phenomena.

ModelIt is an NSF SBIR Phase II funded computational modeling platform, available in English and Spanish, that aligns directly to NGSS Science & Engineering Practice 2 (Developing and Using Models). It complements -- not replaces -- your existing curriculum and CTE pathways by giving students the ability to construct and test their own systems models.

I'd welcome 20 minutes to show you how ModelIt could enhance your Biotech, Aerospace, and Green Engineering pathways while addressing the science proficiency gap.
