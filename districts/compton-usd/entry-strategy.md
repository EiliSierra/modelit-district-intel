## The Hook

"Compton USD was ranked #1 in California for test score gains -- yet science proficiency remains at just 14%, half the state average. Your students are already coding in preschool and building robots across K-12, but they need a tool to BUILD computational models of the scientific phenomena behind the engineering. ModelIt is an NSF-funded, bilingual (EN/ES) platform that lets students develop their own systems models, directly aligned to NGSS Practice 2 -- and it complements your existing Robotics, Biotech, and Green Engineering pathways."

## Decision Process

1. Program identified by department Sr. Director (Torres or Moon)
2. Reviewed by Chief Academic Officer (currently vacant)
3. Piloted at 2-3 schools with teacher PD
4. Results presented to superintendent
5. Budget approved through LCAP or Title funding
6. Board consent calendar approval for purchase
7. District-wide rollout

---

## 5-Phase Approach

### Phase 1: Research & Warm-Up (Weeks 1-4)
- Verify Jorge Torres and Jennifer Moon email addresses from district website
- Research any Digital Promise or NAF connections for warm introductions
- Prepare Compton-specific ModelIt demo with district data points
- Monitor Chief Academic Officer vacancy for new hire announcement

### Phase 2: Initial Outreach (Weeks 5-8)
- Email Jorge Torres with science proficiency data hook
- CC or separate email to Jennifer Moon for elementary angle
- Email Jennifer Graziano with bilingual platform pitch
- Follow up with phone calls within 5 business days

### Phase 3: Demo & Pilot Proposal (Weeks 9-14)
- Schedule virtual or in-person demo at District Office
- Prepare pilot proposal for 2-3 schools (1 elem, 1 middle, 1 high)
- Align pilot with CTE pathways (Biotech, Aerospace, Green Engineering)
- Include teacher PD plan in proposal

### Phase 4: Pilot Execution (Weeks 15-30)
- Launch pilot at selected schools
- Provide teacher training and ongoing support
- Collect student engagement and learning data
- Present mid-pilot progress to stakeholders

### Phase 5: Scale & Adopt (Weeks 31-52)
- Present pilot results to superintendent and cabinet
- Propose district-wide adoption with budget pathway
- Submit for board consent calendar approval
- Plan summer PD for expanded rollout
