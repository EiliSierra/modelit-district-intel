## Key Talking Points

1. **"18% math proficiency demands a different approach"**: "More than 4 out of 5 students aren't meeting math standards. Traditional interventions haven't moved that needle. ModelIt gives students a reason to USE math — they build computational models of systems they can see and touch. It's applied mathematics, not drill-and-kill."

2. **"Agriculture is your launchpad"**: "Valley Center's FFA program is outstanding — State FFA Degrees, hands-on agriculture across the campus. ModelIt turns that agriculture into computational science. Students model crop yields as a function of water, soil, and temperature. They model livestock populations and carrying capacity. They model irrigation systems. It's the bridge between ag practice and data science."

3. **"Solar data → student models"**: "Your ForeFront Power partnership gives students real-time solar production data. Right now they can observe it. With ModelIt, they can BUILD models of energy systems — predicting output based on weather, angle, and season. That's the difference between looking at data and thinking with data."

4. **"Bilingual by design — for your Dual Immersion and EL students"**: "With 24.2% English Learners and an award-winning Dual Immersion program, you value bilingual education. ModelIt is fully bilingual — every student can build models in English or Spanish. Computational thinking transcends language barriers."

5. **"Closest district to Carlsbad in our pipeline"**: "We're based in Carlsbad, about 45 minutes south. That means in-person support, campus visits, and teacher training are easy to schedule. You're not getting a vendor in another state — you're getting a neighbor."

6. **"Culturally responsive STEM"**: "With 8.3% Native American students and MOUs with the Rincon and Pauma bands, cultural responsiveness matters. ModelIt lets students model local ecosystems — the riparian habitats, chaparral biomes, and water systems that are part of Luiseño homeland. It's STEM that respects and builds on your community's knowledge."

7. **"Your new superintendent's math priority"**: "Superintendent Hailwood named math instruction as a top priority for his first year. ModelIt is a tool built for exactly this moment — it embeds mathematical reasoning (variables, equations, graphs, ratios) into authentic problem-solving contexts."

## Objection Handling

| # | Objection | Response |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | "We're focused on chronic absenteeism right now, not curriculum." | "Engaging, project-based learning like ModelIt actually helps attendance. When students are building models of their own agriculture systems or local ecology, they have a reason to show up. Absenteeism and engagement are two sides of the same coin." |
| 2 | "Our students struggle with basic math — they're not ready for modeling." | "ModelIt IS how they learn math. Students who fail abstract math often succeed when they can see the math working in a real system. Building a model of crop yields isn't advanced — it's multiplication, ratios, and graphs in a context that makes sense." |
| 3 | "We just lost an $85M bond measure — we have no money." | "ModelIt is a software license, not a construction project. A pilot is $10-15K — funded from Title I or LCFF Supplemental, which are earmarked funds that must be spent on instruction. You already have the devices (Chromebooks). No infrastructure needed." |
| 4 | "We need to focus on our new superintendent getting settled." | "Superintendent Hailwood has already named math instruction as a priority. Showing him a concrete tool this early helps him build his strategy. A quick demo takes 20 minutes — and he may appreciate having solutions ready when he needs them." |
| 5 | "We already have the ForeFront Power STEAM curriculum." | "Great — ModelIt extends it. ForeFront gives you energy data and monitoring. ModelIt lets students take that data and BUILD computational models of energy systems. It's observation vs. creation — complementary, not competitive." |
| 6 | "How does this help our Native American students?" | "ModelIt's modeling activities can be contextualized to local ecosystems — the chaparral, riparian zones, and wildlife that are part of Luiseño homeland. It's STEM that connects to place and culture. Plus, it's visual and interactive, reducing language barriers." |
| 7 | "We don't have a STEM coordinator to manage this." | "You don't need one. Nicole Clymer at VCHS has NGSS expertise. Your FFA teacher already runs applied science. ModelIt includes teacher training and ongoing support. Two champion teachers is all you need." |
| 8 | "We're losing enrollment to charter schools — we need marketing, not tools." | "The best marketing IS great programs. Parents choose schools based on what's offered. Being able to say 'Our students build computational models of agriculture and ecology' is a differentiator that charter schools can't match." |
