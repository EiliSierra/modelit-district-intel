## The Hook

> "Valley Center-Pauma has an award-winning agriculture program, a bilingual Dual Immersion track, and a brand-new solar STEAM partnership — but only 18% of your students are meeting math standards. ModelIt bridges that gap by turning your students' real-world experiences into computational models. Your FFA students can model crop yields, water cycles, and San Diego County ecosystems. Your Dual Immersion students can build models in English or Spanish. Your ForeFront Power solar data becomes a launching pad for students to model energy systems. With math instruction as a top priority for your first year as superintendent, ModelIt gives your students a reason to USE math by embedding it in the systems they already care about — agriculture, the environment, and their community."

## Decision Process

In a rural-suburban district with a new superintendent:

1. **Superintendent evaluates** → Hailwood is forming his instructional strategy NOW. Position ModelIt as part of his math improvement plan.
2. **Educational Services reviews** → Curriculum director (TBD) evaluates fit with existing programs
3. **CBO confirms funding** → Budget authority identifies Title I, LCFF Supplemental, or CTE grant line
4. **Board approval** → Only if purchase exceeds district threshold (likely for contracts > $10K)
5. **CTE path (alternative)** → FFA/Ag teacher champions ModelIt within agriculture program; CTE funding used
6. **Implementation** → MS/HS science, math, and CTE classes at VCMS and VCHS

**No formal RFP** expected for a pilot-scale purchase. Superintendent has significant authority for instructional material decisions.
