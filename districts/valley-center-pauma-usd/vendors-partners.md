## Vendor Landscape Analysis

### Where ModelIt Fits

| Position | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Direct competitors present** | Unknown — no modeling tools identified in public sources |
| **Complementary tools** | ForeFront Power (energy data), FFA (agriculture), Dual Immersion (bilingual) |
| **Gap ModelIt fills** | Computational modeling + systems thinking — very likely no current tool |
| **Price sensitivity** | HIGH — $85M bond failure signals cost-consciousness; position as high-value supplemental |

### Warm Intro Paths

1. **CSUSM Partnership for Success**: University partnership could provide academic credibility for ModelIt. CSUSM Alliance Advisory Council may include curriculum leaders.

2. **ForeFront Power connection**: The renewable energy curriculum integration shows the district values STEAM partnerships. ModelIt extends this into computational modeling of energy, environment, and sustainability systems.

3. **SDCOE**: San Diego County Office of Education may have STEM coordinators who can introduce ModelIt to multiple districts.

4. **Tribal education channels**: The Rincon Band MOU focuses on academic improvement for tribal students. ModelIt could be positioned as a culturally responsive STEM intervention that serves this population.

### Competitive Threats

| Threat | Level | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Status quo (no new tools) | MEDIUM | New superintendent IS looking to change math outcomes |
| Budget constraints ($85M bond failure) | HIGH | District is cost-conscious; pilot pricing critical |
| Free alternatives (PhET, etc.) | LOW | Free sims don't offer model-building |
| Charter school competition for attention | LOW | Internal focus on enrollment may distract from curriculum adoption |
| Superintendent transition uncertainty | MEDIUM | New leader may need time to establish; but he's already named priorities |
