**Key Demographic Notes**:
- Majority-minority district (63% Hispanic/Latino)
- Significant Native American population (8.3%) — Rincon Band of Luiseño Indians and Pauma Band of Luiseño Indians reservations within district boundaries
- Moderate poverty rate (43.8% economically disadvantaged)
- High EL population (24.2% English Learners) — award-winning Dual Immersion program
- Rural, distant locale covering 300 sq miles in northern San Diego County
- Declining enrollment trend (down from ~4,000+ to 3,636)

### ModelIt Opportunity Analysis

- **Math**: 82% of students NOT meeting math standard — significant improvement opportunity
- **Science**: 81% not meeting science standard — NGSS modeling practice gap
- **ELA-Math gap**: Students perform significantly better in ELA (29%) than math (18%) — math is the acute need
- **New superintendent priority**: Hailwood explicitly named "enhancing math instruction" as a top priority
- **Graduation rate strong**: 94% graduation rate means students persist — they need better math/science instruction, not retention support
- **EL opportunity**: 24.2% English Learners + bilingual ModelIt = natural alignment

## Problems They're Solving

1. **Math/science proficiency gap**: 18% math, 19% science — well below state averages
2. **Chronic absenteeism**: 29.2% of students chronically absent (Hailwood's #1 priority)
3. **Enrollment decline**: Down from 4,000+ to 3,636; competing with charter schools
4. **Facility needs**: $85M bond measure failed in 2024; seeking grants for modernization
5. **Career pathway expansion**: Expanding CTE into culinary, robotics; building on agriculture strength
6. **Tribal student support**: Addressing attendance and academic gaps for Native American students
7. **EL support**: 24.2% English Learners need language-accessible STEM instruction
