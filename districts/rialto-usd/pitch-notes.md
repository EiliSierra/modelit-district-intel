## Key Talking Points

### 1. Math Proficiency Gap
- "Your math proficiency is at 23.73% - about 10 points below the state average. But here's what's interesting: it drops from 34% in grade 3 to just 18% by grade 8. That grade 5-8 window is exactly where computational modeling has the biggest impact."
- "Students aren't just practicing procedures - they're building mathematical models of real phenomena, which means they're using variables, functions, ratios, and data analysis in context."

### 2. STEM CARES Alignment
- "Your students already investigate local environmental issues through STEM CARES. ModelIt gives them the tools to build computer models of those issues - simulate water quality, model air pollution dispersion, test ecosystem interventions - before they act."
- "The environmental stewardship mission + computational modeling = students who can both understand systems scientifically AND propose data-driven solutions."

### 3. NGSS Practice Alignment
- "NGSS Science and Engineering Practice 2 is 'Developing and Using Models.' Practice 5 is 'Using Mathematics and Computational Thinking.' ModelIt directly addresses both."
- "Your science curriculum maps are NGSS-aligned. ModelIt is the tool that makes Practices 2 and 5 happen in the classroom."

### 4. Equity & Bilingual Access
- "With 86% of your students being Hispanic and a significant English Learner population, the bilingual interface matters. Students can toggle between English and Spanish, and the visual nature of modeling transcends language barriers."
- "ModelIt was designed with equity in mind - low floor (any student can start building), high ceiling (advanced students can build complex systems)."

### 5. Complements (Doesn't Replace) Current Curriculum
- "You've invested in Discovery Education and Mystery Science, and those are great platforms. ModelIt doesn't replace them - it extends them. Students watch a DE video about ecosystems, then build a model to test the concepts. They complete a Mystery Science investigation, then model the underlying system."
- "Think of ModelIt as the modeling practice layer on top of your content layer."

### 6. NSF SBIR Proof Point
- "ModelIt is funded by the National Science Foundation through their Small Business Innovation Research program. That means it's been through rigorous scientific review and proven effective in research settings."
- "We're not a startup selling an unproven product - we're a research-backed platform with NSF credibility."

### 7. Cost & Implementation
- "A pilot for 2-3 schools can start at $5K-15K including teacher PD."
- "This fits within Title IV-A (STEM category) or supplemental/concentration funding."
- "We include professional development - teachers get training on how to integrate modeling into their existing curriculum."

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already have Discovery Education" | "Great - ModelIt works alongside DE. Students use DE for content, then ModelIt for the modeling practice. It's the NGSS Practice 2 & 5 tool that complements your content platform." |
| "We don't have budget for new tools" | "A pilot is $5-15K and fits within Title IV-A. Also, your supplemental/concentration funds must be spent on equity-focused programs - ModelIt's bilingual support and equity design make it eligible." |
| "Our teachers are overwhelmed" | "ModelIt comes with PD. And it doesn't add another curriculum - it enriches what teachers are already teaching. We work with existing lessons and units." |
| "We need to focus on math intervention" | "Computational modeling IS math intervention. Students use variables, functions, rates of change, and data analysis in every model they build. It's applied math in context." |
| "We're in the middle of a leadership transition" | "Understood. This is actually a great time because the new LCAP cycle is being developed. Getting ModelIt into the conversation now means it could be a 2026-27 action item." |
| "What's the evidence it works?" | "NSF SBIR Phase II funded after rigorous review. Published research shows significant gains in mathematical reasoning and scientific modeling proficiency. I can share the research summary." |
| "How is this different from PhET simulations?" | "PhET has pre-built simulations students interact with. ModelIt lets students BUILD their own models from scratch - that's the difference between using someone else's model and developing your own, which is what NGSS Practice 2 actually requires." |
