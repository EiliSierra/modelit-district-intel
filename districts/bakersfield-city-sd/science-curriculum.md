## ModelIt Positioning

### Why ModelIt Complements Twig Science
1. **Twig = content delivery**: NGSS-aligned lessons, readings, activities, investigations
2. **ModelIt = computational practice**: Students BUILD their own models (SEP 2: Developing and Using Models + SEP 5: Using Mathematics and Computational Thinking)
3. **Bilingual advantage**: ModelIt's full EN/ES interface serves BCSD's 82.8% Hispanic population and EL students
4. **No overlap**: Twig doesn't offer computational modeling tools; ModelIt doesn't replace curriculum content

### Entry Point
If Twig Science is confirmed, it was likely adopted recently (2022-2024 cycle) -- too soon for replacement. But **supplemental tools can be adopted at any time**. ModelIt enters as a supplemental computational modeling platform, not a curriculum replacement.

LCAP Priority 4 explicitly calls for "increasing access to science and social studies content in all elementary classrooms" -- ModelIt directly fulfills this mandate as a supplemental tool.

### CAST Prep Angle
- **Grade 5 CAST**: 32 elementary schools serve Grade 5 students -- ModelIt provides structured NGSS modeling practice before the first CAST administration
- **Grade 8 CAST**: 10+ junior high/middle schools serve Grade 8 students at 11.18% math proficiency -- computational modeling integrates math into science contexts

### Dual Immersion Connection
- Horace Mann Dual Immersion and other DI sites are natural first pilots
- ModelIt's native Spanish interface is uniquely positioned for Spanish-language science instruction
- Benchmark ELD and English 3D are already in use for EL support -- ModelIt adds bilingual STEM

### KUTR Partnership Angle
- Kern Urban Teacher Residency with CSU Bakersfield trains new teachers in CCSS-Math and NGSS alignment
- ModelIt can be integrated into teacher preparation as an NGSS SEP modeling tool
- New teachers entering BCSD would already be trained on ModelIt
