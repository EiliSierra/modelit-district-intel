## Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We don't have budget" | Title IV-A is specifically for EdTech in well-rounded education — it's already allocated; just needs to be pointed at the right tool |
| "We're already using i-Ready" | i-Ready covers math and reading — science is completely uncovered; ModelIt fills the science gap without replacing anything |
| "We don't have time for another platform" | Zero new login — Canvas + Clever already in use; students access ModelIt through their existing portal |
| "Our teachers are overwhelmed" | ModelIt is a 45-minute session, not a curriculum rewrite. TOSAs (Larry Horton's team) can facilitate the first few sessions |
| "We need to see results first" | Offer a controlled pilot: one science teacher at Haven Drive, one CAST unit, track pre/post simulation scores |
| "Science scores aren't our biggest problem" | "Math is 20% and ELA is 28% — science is 15.71%. The science gap is proportionally deeper, and it's the only subject where you have no dedicated program. The 'Nearly Met' cohort is your fastest ROI." |
