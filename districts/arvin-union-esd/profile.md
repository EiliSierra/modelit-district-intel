**CRITICAL NOTE on Demographic Discrepancy**:
- The CDE baseline provided states SED = 58.9% and EL = 15.5%. However, multiple independent sources (U.S. News, PublicSchoolReview, DonorsChoose) consistently show 94% of students as free/reduced lunch-eligible.
- The CDE district profile page itself reported EL enrollment of 1,814 students = 62.2% of 2,915 enrolled.
- The 58.9% SED figure likely reflects the state-calculated "socioeconomically disadvantaged" definition (which excludes some students who qualify for FRPM but whose parents have a high school diploma), while the 94% figure reflects actual FRPM eligibility.
- **Working figure for LCFF calculation**: 94% economically disadvantaged / high FRPM rate; EL appears to be approximately 15–62% depending on classification method. Recommend verifying unduplicated pupil count from the 2025-26 LCAP.
- **For sales purposes**: Use 94% economically disadvantaged and treat EL population as substantial (the district has a Migrant Education Program, AVID, dual immersion language program, and dedicated EL services).
