## Key Talking Points (7)

1. **"The Nearly Met Opportunity"**
   > "Your CAST data shows 15.71% proficiency — but the story isn't just the 15%. It's the roughly 59% of students who scored 'Nearly Met.' Those students are one tier away from the standard. They understand science content — they just haven't practiced the modeling and data analysis skills CAST actually tests. That's exactly what ModelIt targets."

2. **"No New Technology to Adopt"**
   > "Arvin Union already has Canvas and Clever. ModelIt runs inside Canvas through Clever SSO — your students use their existing login. There's no new portal, no IT project, no training on a new system. It's a new instructional tool inside your existing stack."

3. **"The Only Bilingual Science Modeling Platform"**
   > "For a district that is 92.8% Hispanic with 15.5% English Learners and a Dual Immersion program, the fact that ModelIt has a full English/Spanish interface isn't a selling point — it's a minimum requirement. No other NGSS science modeling platform is built this way."

4. **"Students BUILD — They Don't Just Click"**
   > "Most science simulations are pre-built — students interact with something an adult made. ModelIt is different: students build their own models from scratch, run them, adjust variables, and observe outcomes. This builds the reasoning skills CAST assesses and that NGSS requires as Science and Engineering Practice 2."

5. **"Science Is Your Deepest Gap"**
   > "Math is 20%, ELA is 28%, science is 15.71%. Science is your lowest-performing subject — and it's the only one without a dedicated instructional tool. i-Ready covers math and reading. Science is uncovered. ModelIt fills that gap without displacing anything."

6. **"Title IV-A Is Already There"**
   > "Most districts have Title IV-A (Student Support and Academic Enrichment) allocation. 'Well-Rounded Education Opportunities' under Title IV-A explicitly covers STEM EdTech tools. A pilot for grades 5 and 7-8 typically runs $12,000–$25,000. That's one line item."

7. **"Pilot Results Are Visible Fast"**
   > "With 4 schools and 2,963 students, a Haven Drive pilot with 7th and 8th grade science teachers generates pre/post data in one semester. You don't need a multi-year rollout to see whether it's working. We propose a structured 12-week pilot with pre/post CAST practice assessment comparison."
