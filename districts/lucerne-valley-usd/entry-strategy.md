## The Hook

> "Lucerne Valley has a Bud Biggs STEM Lab, an award-winning FFA program, Destination Imagination teams, and CTE pathways with 80% student participation — but 89% of your students still aren't meeting math standards. ModelIt bridges that gap by turning your students' hands-on STEM experiences into computational models. Your FFA students can model crop yields and desert water cycles. Your Product Design students can simulate engineering systems. Your STEM Lab gets a powerful new software tool that connects hands-on activities to the mathematical reasoning your Dashboard demands. With red indicators on math and ELA, you need interventions that work differently — ModelIt makes math meaningful by embedding it in the real-world systems your students already care about."

## Decision Process

In a small rural district, the decision process is relatively simple:

1. **Director of Academic Programs evaluates** → Aaron Jones identifies ModelIt as a math/science intervention
2. **Superintendent approves** → Peter Livingston signs off on the tool and budget
3. **CBO confirms funding** → Olga Fisher identifies budget line (Title I, CSI, LCFF)
4. **Board approval** → Only if purchase exceeds district threshold
5. **CTE path (alternative)** → Jessica Haecker writes ModelIt into a CTE grant application
6. **Implementation** → MS/HS science/math/CTE teachers in the combined campus

**No RFP process** expected for a small pilot. Superintendent and Director of Academic Programs have authority for instructional material purchases.
