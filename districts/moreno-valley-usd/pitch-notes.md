## Key Talking Points

### 1. Math Proficiency Crisis
- "Your math proficiency is at 18.82% -- about 15 points below the state average. It drops from 30% in Grade 3 to just 14% by Grade 11. That entire trajectory is where computational modeling makes the biggest difference."
- "Students aren't just practicing procedures -- they're building mathematical models of real phenomena, using variables, functions, ratios, and data analysis in context."

### 2. Bilingual Interface = Game Changer
- "With 75.2% Hispanic enrollment and 5,968 English Learners, a tool that only works in English leaves thousands of students behind. ModelIt's full Spanish interface means EL students can engage in STEM while their English catches up."
- "Your DLI program uses a 90/10 model starting in TK. ModelIt can be a STEM resource delivered in Spanish during the target language instructional block."
- "Dr. Ruvalcaba built his career around multilingual learners. This aligns with his personal mission."

### 3. NGSS Practice Alignment
- "NGSS Science and Engineering Practice 2 is 'Developing and Using Models.' Practice 5 is 'Using Mathematics and Computational Thinking.' These are the two hardest practices to implement -- ModelIt was built for exactly this."
- "Whatever science curriculum you've adopted provides content. ModelIt provides the modeling practice layer."

### 4. Middle School STEM Gap
- "Your CTE pathways are strong at the high school level -- cybersecurity, engineering, healthcare. But middle school STEM programming is limited. ModelIt fills that gap as a feeder for your HS engineering and technology pathways."
- "Four middle schools have Engineering/Cyber pathways. ModelIt's computational modeling gives those students the foundational skills before they hit CTE courses."

### 5. Equity & Accessible Design
- "Only 12.83% of African American students are math proficient. Only 5.4% of ELs. Computational modeling is inherently visual and interactive -- students build and test models they can see, not just read about."
- "Low floor, high ceiling: any student can start building a model, advanced students can build complex multi-variable systems."

### 6. Funding Paths
- "You have multiple funding options: LCFF Supplemental/Concentration (high unduplicated %), Title I (all schools), Title III (EL), Title IV-A (STEM), and the newly approved $240M Measure X bond that explicitly includes technology upgrades."
- "A pilot is $10K-25K for 3-5 schools. That's a rounding error in a $608M revenue district."

### 7. NSF Credibility
- "ModelIt is funded by the National Science Foundation through their SBIR program. It's been through rigorous scientific review. We're research-backed, not a startup experiment."
