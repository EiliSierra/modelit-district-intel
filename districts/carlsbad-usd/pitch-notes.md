## Key Talking Points

1. **"California's first K-12 STEM pathway — and ModelIt is the missing computational modeling layer."**
   > Carlsbad built something rare: an articulated PLTW pathway from elementary through high school. But PLTW's own Engineering Design and Development capstone assumes students can build computational models. ModelIt provides exactly that tool — student-built systems models, not teacher-delivered simulations.

2. **"STEAM Labs need instructional programs, not just equipment."**
   > Measure HH is building STEAM Labs at 8 elementary schools. New physical spaces without aligned instructional programs are just rooms. ModelIt gives the labs a clear learning activity structure that's fully NGSS-aligned and ready for immediate use.

3. **"Your EL students score 19% in science. That's a 39-point gap. ModelIt is bilingual."**
   > In a district that's A+ overall, English Learners are still 39 points below average in science. ModelIt's interface is bilingual English/Spanish — students think and model in their primary language while building science concepts in both. No other computational modeling tool on the market is bilingual.

4. **"Students BUILD models, not interact with pre-built ones."**
   > PhET simulations (widely used) give students a simulation that a scientist built. ModelIt gives students a blank canvas to construct their own model from scratch. NGSS SEP 2 explicitly requires students to *develop* models — which means building, not just using. ModelIt is the only tool that operationalizes this at K-12.

5. **"It works on Chromebooks and integrates with Google Workspace."**
   > Carlsbad is an all-Chromebook district with Google Workspace as the LMS. ModelIt runs in-browser on Chromebooks, requires no downloads or special hardware, and can be shared via Google Classroom. Zero IT overhead.

6. **"Middle school math and science dip significantly — ModelIt helps bridge the gap."**
   > Grade 3-4 math proficiency: 75-81%. Grade 7-8 math proficiency: 52-56%. That's a 20+ point drop entering middle school. ModelIt's visual quantitative systems modeling makes abstract relationships concrete — helping students who get lost when math becomes symbolic and disconnected from real phenomena.

7. **"NSF-funded, peer-reviewed, not vaporware."**
   > ModelIt is NSF SBIR Phase II funded — which means it has been independently reviewed for scientific rigor and educational efficacy. This matters for a district that takes NGSS implementation seriously and has a new superintendent who came from a curriculum director role.

| "We already have PLTW — we don't need another STEM tool." | "ModelIt isn't a PLTW replacement — it's the computational modeling layer that PLTW assumes students already have.
